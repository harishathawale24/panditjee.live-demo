Namkaran Puja

"Hindu naming ceremony, 'Namkaran,' signifies religious, social, and legal importance. Held around the twelfth day post-birth, it strengthens familial bonds, introduces faith, and emphasizes parental responsibilities. Namkaran Sanskar Puja shapes the child's character per Vedic guidelines."

Griha Pravesh Puja

"Griha Pravesh Puja, the house inauguration ceremony, occurs before a family enters their new home. A priest selects an auspicious time using astrology. Approva, Sapoorva, and Dwandwah Griha Pravesh are distinct rituals, while online pandits offer convenient services."

Kaal Sarp Dosh Puja

Kalsarp Dosha Nivaran arises when planets align between Rahu and Ketu, causing Kal Sarp Yoga, leading to negative thoughts and fear. An energized yantra, placed correctly, counters it. Kalsarp Pooja has 12 variations for specific manifestations.

Office Opening Puja

Office opening puja is a ceremonial start to new business ventures, symbolizing success and harmony. Through rituals and invocations, it seeks divine blessings, promoting growth, teamwork, and accomplishment in the workspace.

Bhoomi Puja

Choose auspicious months like Shravan, Kartik, Margshirsh, or Paush in the Hindu calendar for land worship before construction. Opt for Mondays or Thursdays, avoiding inauspicious times. Bhoomi pujan follows Vastu Shastra, appeasing positive energies.

Mata ki Chowki

Mata Ki Chowki gathers devotees year-round, spreading Mata's teachings through kirtan sessions. Suitable for joyous occasions, it honors Maa Durga, the revered Mother Goddess, with Vedic Mantras, devotional music, and night-long Jagran.

Krishna Puja

Janmashtami celebrates Lord Krishna's birth, the eighth avatar of Vishnu, born on Ashtami under 'Rohini Nakshatram.' Dwarka's Dwarkadhish temple and Mathura mark this triumph of Dharma over Adharma with joy, 'Makhan Mishri,' and 'Handi Fod' festivities.

Shiv Puja

Shiva Puja, especially on Shivaratri, seeks to appease Lord Shiva, known as "The Destroyer." Worshiping the Shiva Linga with diverse traditional rituals, including abhisheka, mantra, tantra, kriyas, and mudras, varies among sects.

Navratri Puja

Ghatasthapana, a vital Navratri ritual, invokes Goddess Shakti with strict timing rules. Avoiding Amavasya and nighttime, it must be done during the first one-third of the day on Pratipada, or Abhijit Muhurta if necessary. Nakshatra Chitra and Vaidhriti Yoga, while advised against, aren't strictly prohibited. Crucially, Ghatasthapana should be finished before Hindu midday during Pratipada.
